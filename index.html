Social Distancing in New York City Parks During the Coronavirus Pandemic

by Radhamely De Leon

November 6, 2020

New York City parks employees were asked to advise crowds they encountered to adhere to social distancing guidelines.

According to data provided by the city’s parks department, groups encountered in Manhattan parks were more likely to comply with employee’s advice than in other boroughs. But crowds across all five boroughs were more likely to ignore instruction and not comply.

“If this is a family people who live in together and simply go to a park and stay together, it's definitely less dangerous,” says Eugene Chudnovsky, a professor of physics and astronomy from Lehman College who studied the effects of ventilation systems on coronavirus infections. “But if they mix with people from different houses in the park and they [are] close to each other, then again, it depends on many factors, like if it's windy. The wind is constantly taking away the virus and then the probability of getting sick is very small.”

Data from New York City Parks, Maintenance & Operations division
